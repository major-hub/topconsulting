  • Russia

    Russia - Russia (Russian Federation) - the largest state in the world, which occupies 1/6 of the entire land area of the Earth. It is a country with a long history, rich culture and generous nature. Some architectural and historical and cultural monuments on the territory of the Russian Federation are included in the list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Russia is the homeland of great poets and writers, artists and architects, scientists, musicians, composers, cosmonauts and travelers. Russian painting, classical music and ballet are part of the world cultural heritage. Russia is located in the Northern Hemisphere, in the north of the Eurasia continent. Conventionally, the borders between Europe and Asia run along the Ural Mountains, the Ural River and the Caspian Sea, therefore part of the country is located in Europe, part in Asia. Russia shares borders with eighteen countries. It has land borders with Norway, Finland, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Poland, Belarus, Ukraine, Abkhazia, Georgia, South Ossetia, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, China, Mongolia and North Korea. Sea borders - with Japan and the USA. The climate of Central Russia is characterized by slight temperature differences. In winter, the average temperature here is -10 ° С, and in summer - + 20 ° С. In Western Siberia, in winter, the air temperature can drop to -40 ° С, and in summer, on average, + 20 ° С. The lowest temperatures are in Eastern Siberia. In the village of Oymyakon in Yakutia, the lowest temperature was noted (-72 ° C). In summer, it is cool and humid here, the average temperature is + 19 ° С.

  • Poland

    Poland is a state in Eastern Europe. It is located mostly in the Central European Plain, as well as in the northeastern part of the mountainous regions of Central Europe. From the north it is washed by the Baltic Sea. The total area of Poland is 312 658 km² (in terms of area it ranks 69th in the world and 9th in Europe). Climate. The climate of Poland is temperate, transitional from maritime to continental. Climatic conditions are influenced by colliding ocean masses from the west, cold air from Scandinavia and Russia, and warmer subtropical air from the south. Currency. Zloty (PLN). Currency can be exchanged at specialized exchange offices (Kantor wymiany walut or Kantor), no commission is charged. "Exchangers" are usually located at train stations or in the historic center of the city (as a rule, they are not in banks). Banks are open from 8:30 am to 1:00 pm on weekdays (central offices until 5:00 pm), some are open until 6:00 pm. Exchange offices usually work until 19: 00-20: 00, and even longer in large tourist centers. Credit cards are accepted in many hotels and restaurants, car rental companies, etc.

  • Turkey

    Turkey is a country of ancient traditions and customs. A country of ancient cities filled with modern oriental flavor, elders in red frescoes, black-haired beauties in dresses made of beads and pearls, the smell of freshly baked bread, oriental music and dances - all this is Turkey. This country is attractive because it provides a wide variety of opportunities for active or relaxing, chic or low-budget, large company or romantic and secluded recreation. This country, which is open to guests all year round: in summer, rest on the beaches of the Mediterranean and Aegean Seas, in winter ski resorts, throughout the year - historical excursions, adventure tours, shopping. Climate and weather in Turkey Turkey's climate varies by region. In the west and south, dominated by a Mediterranean climate, the summer months are hot and dry and the winters mild. The temperature of the water and air on the Mediterranean coast allows swimming and sunbathing from April to October. But the climate of the Aegean coast is a little milder, the air temperature here is 3-5 degrees lower and the air humidity is less. The swimming season is also from April to October. Currency of Turkey The monetary unit of Turkey is the Turkish lira. Money can be exchanged everywhere, but private exchange offices usually offer better rates than banks or hotels. When exchanging, please note that the exchange office may take a commission for each operation (there should be a corresponding announcement about this).

  • Malaysia

    Malaysia is an amazing country of unusual combinations, where in the capital you can find a traditional Malay village, squeezed by skyscrapers; where huge multi-colored butterflies sit on the hoods of expensive cars, and a king with a magnificent title signs bills proposed by parliament. Malaysia, which gained independence only in 1957, is today an actively developing country, the industrial sector of which makes up about half of the GDP; in second place is the sphere of services and tourism. It is curious that Malaysia is the world leader in the production of certain types of household appliances and electronics. The motley ethnic composition, friendly coexistence of different nationalities with their own traditions and customs, combined with the unique nature, make Malaysia especially attractive for tourists from all over the world. Malaysia is a federal elective constitutional monarchy, consisting of three federal territories and thirteen states, nine of which are monarchies, whose hereditary rulers (sultans) are also the spiritual heads of the states. Once every five years, they elect the Supreme Ruler of the country - the king (his title in Malay sounds like "Yang di-Pertuan Agong"), who performs representative functions. The real government of the country is in the hands of the parliament and the cabinet of ministers. Climate The country is characterized by a hot and humid equatorial climate dependent on monsoons. During the day and throughout the year, the air temperature fluctuates very slightly - from 25 ° to 28 ° C, only in lowlands, and especially river ones, can exceed 30 ° C. Air humidity in the rainy season reaches 90% - but the showers are mostly short-term (no more than two hours). On the east coast of Malacca (West Malaysia), in the west of Sarawak and in the northeast of Sabah, the rainy season is from October-November to February; on the west coast (Langkawi, Penang, Pangkor islands) - in April-May and from October to December. There are no seismically active zones on the territory of the country - this factor and temperature stability allow tourists to plan a trip to Malaysia at any time of the year. Currency The national currency of Malaysia is the ringgit (denoted as MYR); banknotes in denominations of 1, 5, 10, 20, 50 and 100 ringgit. The ringgit is divided into 100 sen - coins of 1, 5, 10, 20, 50 sen are used. Dollars, euros, British pounds sterling can be exchanged at all Malaysian commercial banks and some hotels. Banks are open on weekdays from 9:15 am to 4:30 pm (some are open until 4:00 pm).   • Great Britain

    Great Britain's official name is the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland - an island state in northwest Europe. It consists of four "historic provinces": England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland. The state is located in the British Isles. The area of Great Britain is 243 809 km², the southern coast is connected to continental Europe through a 50 km long Eurotunnel (of which 38 km are under water). This is the longest underwater tunnel in the world. Northern Ireland shares a 360 kilometer land border with the Republic of Ireland and is the UK's only land border. The Greenwich Observatory in London is the site of the prime meridian. Climate Great Britain has a temperate oceanic climate with a lot of rainfall throughout the year. Temperatures vary with the seasons, but rarely drop below -11 ° C or rise above 35 ° C. The main winds come from the southwest and often bring cold and wet weather from the Atlantic Ocean, but the eastern parts of the country are mostly sheltered from these winds, and since most of the rainfall occurs in the western regions, the eastern ones are the driest. The Atlantic currents, warmed by the Gulf Stream, bring mild winters, sometimes snowfalls in winter and early spring, although snow usually does not last long. Currency unit Pound sterling, pound, British pound (£). £ 1 is sibdivided into 100p. You can change your currency at banks that are open Monday through Friday from 9:30 am to 3:30 pm (large banks are open until 5:30 pm), as well as at currency exchange offices, which often work around the clock. It is worthwhile to evaluate in advance the profitability of exchanging money at a bank or point, comparing the rate and commission.

    Canada is a state of the British Commonwealth, and formally the head of state is the Queen of England. The Queen's official representative in Canada is the Governor General, who is appointed by the Queen on the advice of the Prime Minister. Canada is a state located in North America. Language Canada has two official languages - English and French. English is the first language of 16.1 million Canadians, and French is the first language of 6.6 million. For many Canadians, their native languages are Italian, Chinese, German, Portuguese, Polish, Ukrainian, Danish, Greek and others. Canada is the best country to live in For several years in a row, Canada has been recognized as the best country to live in by the UN Commission. The choice is made on the basis of many indicators, which include the health care system, the quality and availability of education, the cleanliness of the environment, protected by the law of individual freedoms, and the average level of wages in relation to expenses. This can only mean one thing: Canada is a country where people with high incomes live, where people are tolerant of each other, where the government provides free, but at the same time, high-quality medical care, where they provide free secondary education, and where it is good to live among friendly people. Living standards in Canada are considered one of the highest in the world. If a person agrees to work, then his future and the future of his children in Canada are guaranteed. In 1986, the World Bank estimated that every average Canadian is practically a millionaire. The average Canadian has 960,000 Canadian dollars on hand. Canada is one of the most environmentally friendly countries in the world. In Canada, you can safely drink tap water, without thinking about the consequences associated with Chernobyl or other contamination still unknown to us.

Services and Programs

Summer schools

Summer schools (in Britain they are called Summer School) is a program, usually lasting 2 weeks or more, during which attendees are usually offered intensive study of certain subjects in combination with extracurricular activities. As the name suggests, these courses are traditionally organized in the summer between school or university terms. In most cases, they involve boarding.

The Farabi Exchange Program

The Farabi Exchange Program is a student and teacher exchange program between higher education institutions, providing education and training at the junior, senior, graduate and doctoral levels at universities and high technology institutes. The Farabi exchange program aims to enable students or teachers to continue their studies and studies at a higher education institution outside their educational institution for one or two semesters.

Preparatory courses

Preparatory courses (sub. Courses) - this is the zero course of the undergraduate program. Designed for group additional training in major disciplines before entrance exams to a higher educational institution. Students in preparatory courses are called applicants. Most often, classes are held on weekdays in the evening, less often on Saturdays. The volume of courses can be about 4 classroom hours per week for several months. Usually, training is conducted on the educational premises of the university.

Language courses

An educational course in which a foreign language is taught.

Bachelor

Bachelor is a full-fledged higher education. In the diploma of such a graduate it will be written: "A bachelor's qualification (degree) has been awarded in such and such a direction."

Master's degree

Master's degree is another two years at the institute. It is provided for those who want to go to science or become a teacher.

PhD

PhD is an academic degree of a doctor of philosophy or other specialties not related to philosophy (here we mean the totality of sciences, not a specialty), which is awarded in America, Canada, Germany or other Western countries and is the last stage of education.

Foundation

Foundation is a program for preparing applicants for a university, helping them to adapt in the country of study, to improve their level of foreign language proficiency and acquire additional academic skills to meet all the requirements of study in a higher education institution.

Erasmus Exchange Program

Erasmus Exchange Program - Through the Erasmus program, students and employees can gain a significant advantage in the business world, as well as have work experience abroad, and they can make their resumes more attractive on their business platforms by increasing their foreign language proficiency.

  • What is apostille?

    international standardized filling form information about the legality of the document for presentation to territories of countries that recognize this form of legalization. The Apostille stamp is placed on originals and copies of documents.

  • Where can I get an apostille?

    state bodies authorized to affix apostille in Uzbekistan, these are, first of all, various ministries: Ministry of Justice of the Republic of Uzbekistan, Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Uzbekistan; as well as the General Prosecutor's Office Of the Republic of Uzbekistan and the State Testing Center under the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan.

  • Why do you need an apostille?

    such a form is documented and guaranteed to confirm that your document is legal and can be used for outside the Republic of Uzbekistan.

  • What does an apostille look like?

    the apostille form is the same in all countries: in the "cap" the document has the word "Apostille" in French, followed by the inscription “Convention de la Haye du 5 octobre 1961 "or" The Hague Convention of October 5, 1961 ".

  • Where is the apostille affixed?

    the apostille stamp can be placed as on the original document, and on its notarized copy (for example, on a birth certificate in Uzbekistan).
